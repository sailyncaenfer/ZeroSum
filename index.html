How ZeroSum Works

Swipe (or use arrow keys / WASD) to slide every tile in one direction. Tiles glide until they hit the edge of the 4×4 board or another tile.

Tiles

5 Black tiles hold positive numbers. 5 White tiles hold negative numbers. Every tile is drawn from a shuffled deck of the values −10 through 10 (0 is never dealt on its own), so you'll always see a mix of both colors.

Merging

When a move pushes two tiles together, they merge only if both of these are true:

  • Their values add up to something between −10 and 10.
  • Either one of the two tiles has an absolute value of 3 or less, or the two values are exact opposites (they sum to zero).

So a small tile can combine with almost anything nearby, while two big tiles only merge if they perfectly cancel out.

Scoring

Merges that don't cancel out just combine into a new tile carrying the sum — no points for those. The real reward is a zero-sum merge: when two tiles sum to exactly 0, they both vanish and you score 10 × their absolute value.

New Tiles & Game Over

After every successful move, one new tile appears in a random empty cell. The game ends when the board is full and no move in any direction would allow a tile to merge. Press R or tap Restart any time to start a fresh game.
