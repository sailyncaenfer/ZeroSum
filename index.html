How ZeroSum Works

Swipe (or use arrow keys / WASD) to slide every tile in one direction. Tiles glide until they hit the edge of the 4×4 board or another tile.

Tiles

5 Black tiles hold positive numbers. 5 White tiles hold negative numbers. Every tile is drawn from a shuffled deck of the values −10 through 10 (0 is never dealt on its own), so you'll always see a mix of both colors.

Cancelling

When a move pushes two tiles together, they interact only if they're exact opposites — equal magnitude, mismatched color, like 7 and 7. That pair cancels out completely and both tiles vanish from the board. Any other pairing — same value, same color, or unrelated numbers — just blocks and stacks up without merging.

Scoring

Each cancellation scores 10× the tile's magnitude. Cancelling a pair of 7s scores 70 points.

Next Tiles

The column beside the board previews the next three tiles that will be dealt in. Tiles are drawn from a shuffled bag of every value from −10 to 10 (excluding 0); once a bag runs out, a fresh one is shuffled. A dashed line in the preview marks the start of that next bag.

Game Over

After every successful move, one new tile appears in a random empty cell. The game ends when the board is full and no move in any direction would allow a cancellation. Press R or tap Restart any time to start a fresh game.
